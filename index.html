EMOTIONAL INTELLIGENCE & LEADERSHIP

Through recent reflections, I’ve realized that both emotional intelligence and motivation deeply influence how I work and lead. From the emotional intelligence perspective, I learned that **empathy is one of my greatest strengths**—I naturally create harmony and support others’ ideas. However, I also noticed a gap: I often hesitate to voice my own opinions, fearing conflict. This made me understand that true leadership requires balancing empathy with **assertiveness** to make confident contributions.

MOTIVATION & PURPOSE

Similarly, exploring motivation theories, especially **Herzberg’s Two-Factor Theory**, helped me see that recognition, trust, and responsibility drive me far more than external rewards. My internship experience, where I was trusted with autonomy, showed me how **purpose and appreciation fuel high performance**. Conversely, a lack of recognition during group work revealed how poor communication and unclear roles can reduce engagement.

**<CONCLUSION>** Effective leadership grows from inner motivation and emotional balance—feeling trusted, valued, and confident while creating the same environment for others. My goal is to build spaces where empathy and ownership coexist, turning motivation into a shared force that sustains team success.
